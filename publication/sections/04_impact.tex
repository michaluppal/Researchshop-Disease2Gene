\section{Impact}
\label{sec:impact}

\subsection{Key Contributions}

ResearchShop addresses the practical scale mismatch between biomedical literature and manual gene-evidence review. PubMed now indexes more than 40 million biomedical and life-science citations~\cite{pubmed_about_2026}; ResearchShop does not replace curation, but turns selected open-access papers into auditable gene--evidence tables that reviewers can inspect.


\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Hybrid extraction with auditable validation.} PubTator3, HGNC scanning, and configurable LLM extraction surface candidate genes, while grounding, citation checks, and confidence gates decide what reaches the primary export. Output rows include candidate source, normalization, validation outcome, confidence score, association type, and paper metadata; audit files preserve pre-validation candidates and gate drops.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Applications}

\begin{itemize}[itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS) evidence triage:} Helps researchers identify and review published gene-disease evidence relevant to variant interpretation.
    \item \textbf{Rare disease evidence triage:} Helps process curated PMID sets or focused PubMed searches where relevant reports are sparse and require source-level review.
    \item \textbf{Drug target discovery:} Helps researchers assemble candidate gene--disease association tables for therapeutic-development review.
    \item \textbf{Systematic reviews:} Produces auditable extraction tables that can support, but not replace, documented review and adjudication workflows.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Free-tier Execution Study}

We ran the same 10 open-access PMIDs hourly for 24 Warsaw-local slots on a Windows/WSL laptop using \texttt{gemini-3.1-flash-lite}, one worker, and a three-call cap per paper. This is an operational reliability check, not a clinical accuracy benchmark.

\subsection{Limitations}

The LLM used in the pipeline is non-deterministic, which limits strict reproducibility of the system. At its current stage, ResearchShop relies on PubMed-based discovery, open-access full-text availability, and the Gemini API, all of which may introduce additional biases or limitations. Rare-disease searches require particular care: the topic-search ranking uses citation and journal-quality signals that can deprioritise low-citation rare-disease papers, so users studying rare conditions should prefer direct PMID submission or carefully documented focused PubMed queries. Architecturally, however, the system remains modular and can be extended to alternative search sources or local models in future versions. Consequently, outputs generated by ResearchShop should be treated as a starting point for further investigation and always subjected to professional review and verification.
