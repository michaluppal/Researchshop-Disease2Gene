## Annotation Policy

This is a focused fixture for later final CSV row comparison, not a complete gene catalogue. Genes are included when the paper makes them part of a central MIS-C or pediatric COVID-19 immunopathological signature and the marker maps cleanly to an HGNC gene symbol.

Interpretation rules:

- Use signature, biomarker, repertoire expansion, genetic susceptibility, or association wording.
- Do not convert protein biomarker findings into causal gene-disease claims.
- For HLA entries, require allele-combination context: HLA-A*02, HLA-B*35, HLA-C*04.
- For TRBV11-2, require TCR repertoire usage/expansion context, preferably early/transient MIS-C context.
- For cytokines and chemokines, accept protein/soluble-biomarker evidence only if grounded to the paper's MIS-C inflammatory signature.

## Excluded Or Secondary Markers

These are intentionally not expected final CSV genes for this focused fixture:

- **IFN-alpha2a / IFNA2:** central to pCOVID-19 type I IFN response, but not in the selected MIS-C-focused expected set.
- **IL-33 / IL1RL1:** important low/sST2 matrisome context, but excluded to avoid broad panel exhaustiveness.
- **TNFRSF1A/TNFRSF1B, IL2RA, IL17A:** soluble receptor/cytokine panel markers, not selected as core expected outputs.
- **VEGFA, VCAM1, SELE:** endothelial injury protein biomarkers; relevant context but outside the focused list.
- **LTF:** neutrophil biomarker; MPO retained as representative.
- **Ferritin:** clinical protein/lab marker with subunit ambiguity, not a clean single expected gene.
- **CRP and NPPB:** clinical/proteomic inflammation or cardiac involvement markers, not central immune-gene signatures.
- **TROVE2 and ATP4A:** autoantibody targets with IVIG confounding; not expected gene-disease findings.
- **CD28 and SLAMF1:** surface protein markers on TRBV11-2 CD4+ T cells; secondary to the repertoire finding.
- **HCST, DUSP2, GZMK, PRF1, GZMA, IL32, PSMB1, HAVCR2, SIRPG, CTLA4, RAC2, MSN, ITGB2, SELL, IL2RB, IL2RG:** secondary CITE-seq expression/pathway markers for TRBV11-2 CD4+ T-cell activation and effector state.

## Scoring Notes

A row should be considered correct when it grounds the gene to the paper's reported biomarker/signature context. A row should be considered incorrect or overclaimed when it asserts mutation, pathogenic variant, direct causality, or gene-disease determinism unsupported by the paper.

For HLA rows, the allele combination is the finding. Separate HLA-A, HLA-B, or HLA-C rows without A*02/B*35/C*04 context should receive weaker credit.

For TRBV11-2, the paper supports transient expansion/usage in MIS-C and association with inflammation/T-cell activation; it does not report a TRBV11-2 mutation.
